Preparation

We have divided our preparation strategy into three parts for effective and successful cold calling services.

Step 1: Identifying the product

In the first part, we focus on the supplier or product or the service that is to be represented. We choose to represent products/services that fit a specific purpose for a particular market or application. This helps make our opening calls stronger.

Once the product that is to be represented is decided, our next step is to ensure that the organisation we are representing is ethical, socially responsible and conforms with the expectations of its target market. If it fails to meet any of the criteria, as your representative, our team of callers will not have strong foundations/propositions to build-on and market the product or service in a convincing way. In this case, we would engage in a discussion to suggest an alternative product or service to represent.

While the above steps detail our approach to cold calling services, here's a deeper look at our best practices.

Introduction

A good introduction during a cold call service can go a long way in converting your prospects into clients. When talking to customers, we make sure that they are clear about who we are and the purpose with which we have called them. This helps set the stage to request dialogue instantly or schedule the call to a later time according to the availability of the person. Our opening proposition focuses entirely on a detailed pitch of your product offering. Our team members are well trained to make it concise and to the point.

Right questioning

We prepare facilitative questions that are designed to help customers have a better and clearer view of the situation. We frame and pose questions in such a way that they invite customers into considering your product/service and then create the right opening to explain how the product can help solve their issues.

Impartiality

As a cold calling service, we stress on impartiality. It is a tricky thing to do in a business that involves the selling of products and services, but remaining fair and neutral is a very vital trait in this line of work.

You may have come across sellers who criticize and condemn their competitors. By doing this, they are only damaging their brand's reputation. We often remind our team of cold calling experts that people are always looking for an expert in a particular field who could help and guide them to make an informed decision, instead of someone who is trying to sell them something. A good conversation means a loyal customer.

Listening from Customers' Perspective

Our experts are trained to listen and interpret the needs from the perspective of the customer instead of acting as biased or one-sided self-interested sales people. They understand that prospects can immediately judge whether a caller's actions are intended to be helpful or selfish.

Educating Effectively

Our callers are completely aware of the service, proposition or technology they are representing.They possess the knowledge to give customers fair information when they ask about something and offer them useful feedback in response to their requests. We understand rushing in to close the appointment won’t do any good. Our goal is always to understand our customers and identify whether your product is a valuable fit for what they need.

Involving Customers in The Process

As a part of cold calling lead generation services, we try and involve the customers in our discussion. We let the customer guide the conversation while at the same time keeping the conversation focussed on the product. By acting as the bridge as well as the interpreter we ease customers into a conversation in a very natural manner.

Staying in Touch with Prospects

During cold calls, our team members make it a point to take notes and keep records of the call at all stages. We understand that we have a responsibility towards all the relationships that we initiate and we have witnessed numerous instances where customers have appreciated us staying in touch thus catapulting our clients' brand value.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does cold calling actually work?

Cold calling helps you connect with your customer in the most productive and personal way. It helps you to acquire new customers, understand your target audience better, and build long-lasting business relationships. Our cold calling services can help you:

  • Define your markets
  • Investigate any business and their human resource requirements
  • Carry out lead generation campaigns to capture prospects
  • Carry out email campaigns
  • Create actionable calling scripts
How many cold calls can you make in an hour?

We can make about 8 cold calls per hour.

What is the best time to make cold calls?

The optimal time for making cold calls is 8 and 9 am in the morning and 4 and 5 pm in the evening.

What is a good cold call conversion rate?

Since most people find cold calling intrusive, a good conversion rate for cold calls is around 2%

What is better cold calling or emailing?

Cold calling is more personal when it comes to initiating conversations with your prospects when compared to email. It can be used to get referrals, schedule meetings, or even close a sale right there.

On the other hand, it is convenient, cost-effective and less intrusive to contact people through email. Emails allow your customers to read your message at their convenience.

In closing, both these approaches need to work in tandem to help retain and grow your business' customer base. Cold calls are great for initiating personal conversations with a potential customer, while emails can act as follow-ups in the form of reminders, sale announcements, etc.

Is it really necessary to have a prepared script?

Having a ready script at your disposal has a lot of benefits when it comes to cold calling. A brief and crisp telephone script creates a really good first impression. It makes you sound more professional and helps you to meet your brand standards. Scripts also help reduce the stress and anxiety of how to take the call forward. Finally, it yields better results by increasing productivity.
